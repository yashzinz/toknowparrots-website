Peek Into My Daily Life

Sprinkle & Sing
There's nothing better than a refreshing mist on my feathers while grooving to some tunes. Yes, I love getting sprinkled or hopping into the shower when the music is on. You'll find me flapping, singing, and fluffing — it's my version of karaoke and spa day rolled into one!
Busy Beak, Happy Heart
I’m always up to something! My days are filled with chewable wood toys, colorful ropes, puzzle feeders, shreddables, and even noisy trinkets. Foraging toys challenge my brain, and climbing ropes keep me acrobatic. Enrichment is how I stay smart, active, and out of mischief!
Fresh, Fruity Mornings
I start my day with a rainbow! My breakfast bowl is filled with fresh veggies and fruits such as pomegranate, blueberries, drumsticks, long beans, red chillies, dragon fruit, guava, apple, oranges, strawberries, pears, mangoes, kusa, cluster beans, and beet — all chopped just right. After that, I get some healthy pellets to crunch on. Best part? I love earning my favorite bits through fun tricks like recall!

Keshav’s Journey

A heartfelt timeline of resilience, healing, and love.

09-12-2022 – First Day Home
From Silence and Fear
Keshav entered our lives as a fragile 7-month-old baby bird from a pet shop. He couldn’t fly, and his fear was heartbreaking — screaming if anyone approached him. We didn’t know then that a seed-only diet wasn’t ideal for him, and unknowingly, we began with a lot of mistakes. Still, we hoped love would be enough.
23-12-2022 – A Sister’s Love
When Soham Arrived
Just weeks later, we brought home Soham — a gentle, cheerful girl who was Keshav’s polar opposite. She quickly grew fond of us, always eager to play and explore. Keshav, meanwhile, stayed distant and reserved, often watching quietly from afar. But having Soham nearby brought a new energy into the home — a hope that maybe Keshav, too, would open up one day.
09-02-2023 – Saying Goodbye
A Sudden Loss
Tragedy struck unexpectedly — Soham fell ill and passed away within hours at the vet. Her death was devastating. She had brought so much joy in her short life. We buried her by a quiet beach, where the sky stretched wide — a place where she could fly forever free. Her loss made us realize how little we knew, and how much we needed to change.
13-04-2023 – The First Connection
Trust, Slowly Gained
Motivated by Soham’s memory, we began transforming how we cared for Keshav. We studied, consulted with vets, and began feeding fresh fruits, vegetables, and sprouted grains. I sat with him daily, softly talking, offering treats from a distance, and playing music. One quiet morning, Keshav climbed onto my shoulder and gently took a treat. That small gesture was a breakthrough — the beginning of a deep, mutual trust.
23-07-2023 – First Flight
Wings Unfold
Months of patience, love, and a proper diet finally bore fruit — Keshav’s flight feathers regrew. And then, it happened: he took his very first full flight around our home. It was beautiful and indescribable. He had endured fear, trauma, and loss — but in that moment, he was truly free. His feathers grew vibrant, and with each flap, so did his confidence.
11-08-2023 – Shower Time Joy
Music, Mist, and Happiness
For the first time, Keshav genuinely enjoyed being showered. We misted him gently with a plant bottle while soft music played, and he fluffed up, danced, and flapped joyfully. It became our weekly ritual — his happy showers, always paired with music. It’s one of his favorite activities now, and ours too.
28-04-2024 – A Bright Mind
Entertainment & Training
We discovered Keshav’s love for watching Amazon parrots on YouTube and parrot videos on his little tablet. He’d watch with curiosity and sometimes even chirp back. Around this time, we also began formal training — starting with “step-up” using a stick, and then moving to recall training. Today, he flies to my hand when called, eager for his favorite treat and a cuddle.
26-06-2024 – The World Outside
Tiny Steps Toward Adventures
Wanting to take him on safe, enriching walks, we started desensitizing him to a carrier bag. At first, he was hesitant — strangers and the outdoors still scare him. But slowly, we’re building up his comfort so one day, he can safely visit parks or go for gentle walks in the winter sun. It’s a work in progress — but we’re in no rush. Every step is worth celebrating.
